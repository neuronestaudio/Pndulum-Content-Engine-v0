**SLIDE 2 — THE ASSUMPTION**
- Label: `WHAT YOU'VE BEEN TOLD`
- Headline: **"MORE CLICKS = MORE JOBS."**
- Body: Every ad report leads with click-through rate. It feels like progress — the line is going up, the agency looks busy. But a click is someone glancing at your ad. It is not a booked car in your bay.

**SLIDE 3 — THE DIAGNOSIS**
- Label: `YOU'RE WATCHING THE WRONG LINE`
- Headline: **CLICKS ARE A VANITY METRIC.**
- Body: The only number that pays your rent is **cost per booked job.** When eBay switched off its search ads in a controlled test, sales were *statistically indistinguishable from zero change* — the clicks were never the cause of the sales. *(Blake, Nosko & Tadelis, Econometrica 2015)*

**SLIDE 4 — THE EVIDENCE (YOUR OWN ACCOUNT)**
- Label: `WE SEE IT IN REAL ACCOUNTS`
- Big stat: **CTR ↑2× · COST/JOB ↑8×**
- Body: One live detailer account: click-through rate climbed from **4.1% to 8.2%** over the run — *twice as good* on paper. In the same window, cost per booked job went from **A$5.81 to A$50**, and bookings fell from **31 a week to 7.** The "better" metric was hiding a collapse. *(Live AU detailer account, 2026)*

**SLIDE 5 — WHAT THE GIANTS KNOW (CASE STUDY)**
- Label: `WHAT THE GIANTS KNOW · P&G`
- Big stat: **–$200M, $0 LOST**
- Body: The world's biggest advertiser, Procter & Gamble, cut **US$200 million** of digital ad spend after finding much of it was wasted impressions. Sales didn't drop — and reach actually rose ~10%. The spend was buying *metrics*, not customers. *(Adweek / The Drum, 2018)*

**SLIDE 6 — HONEST COUNTERPOINT / COST**
- Label: `THE HONEST COUNTERPOINT`
- Headline: **CTR ISN'T USELESS — IT'S JUST NOT THE GOAL.**
- Body: A healthy click rate is a fine *early* signal that your ad and audience match. The trap is optimising for it. A high CTR on the wrong searches just means you're *efficiently* buying clicks that never book — paying more per job for a prettier chart.
- Honest line: *Watch CTR. Pay your bills with cost-per-booked-job.*

**SLIDE 7 — DO IT MONDAY**
- Label: `STEAL THIS · DO IT MONDAY`
- Headline: **MEASURE WHAT PAYS.**
- `01` Track one number weekly: **cost per booked job** (spend ÷ actual bookings), not clicks or CTR.
- `02` Watch for **scaling decay** — the first ad dollars book cheap, the next don't. Cap spend where your cost-per-job breaks.
- `03` Kill any metric on your report that can't be traced to a job in the diary.
- Line: *You can pull all three from the account you already have — no new spend.*
