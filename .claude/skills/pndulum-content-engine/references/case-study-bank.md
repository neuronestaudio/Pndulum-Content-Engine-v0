# Big-Name Case-Study Bank

Each post anchors ONE familiar big-name corporation with backed data. Always
**re-verify the number with a live search before publishing** — figures drift
and sources matter. Below are vetted anchors with their best stat and angle.

| Giant | Stat (verify) | Angle / best-fit topic | Source family |
|-------|---------------|------------------------|---------------|
| **Amazon** | Prime converts ~74% vs ~13% non-members (~22× the ~3.3% retail avg) | You don't need more traffic — convert what you have | Millward Brown Digital, 2015 |
| **Amazon** | Every 100ms latency ≈ 1% of sales | Speed = revenue | Greg Linden, ~2006 |
| **Tesla** | Factory paint 30–50% thinner/softer; owners spend up to 300% more on paint repair; Model 3/Y most-PPF'd | EV growth → more protection demand | Detailing industry data, 2025 |

| **Starbucks** | Rewards drives a large share of US sales; loyalty lifts spend/visit | Hidden revenue in existing customers | Starbucks IR (verify yearly) |
| **Salesforce / HubSpot** | Bots cut first-response time 54%; lifted qualified conversion 3%→5% | Automate the first reply | Salesforce/HubSpot data |
| **Bain & Co** | +5% retention → +25–95% profit | Retention > acquisition | Bain / HBR |

## Sourcing rules
- Prefer primary or well-known secondary sources; name them on-slide.
- One giant per post. Don't reuse the same giant on back-to-back posts unless
  the angle is genuinely different (Amazon-latency vs Amazon-conversion is OK).
- If a stat can't be verified live, drop it — never approximate.
